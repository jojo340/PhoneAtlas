## 2. Core App Features

The App provides smartphone catalog browsing, filtering, favorites, side-by-side specification comparison, and catalog database updates. Users do not need to create an account or provide a name, phone number, email address, contacts, or similar personal information to use the main features.

## 3. Information We Collect Directly

The App does not currently require account registration and does not ask users to submit identity information.

The following information is primarily stored locally on the user’s device:

- Favorited phone models
- Phones added to comparison
- Selected in-app language
- Cached phone catalog data

This information is used to provide favorites, comparison, language preference, and cached/offline browsing experiences. Unless the user backs up or syncs the device through system-level services, the App does not upload these local preferences to a developer-operated server.

## 4. Remote Catalog Updates

When the user taps the catalog update button, the App sends a network request to the configured remote JSON data source to download the latest phone catalog file. The remote service provider, such as GitHub or another hosting provider, may receive standard network information such as IP address, request time, and network environment. Such information is handled by the relevant service provider under its own privacy policy.

## 5. Advertising and Third-Party SDKs

The App uses Google AdMob to display ads. Depending on service configuration, user consent status, system permissions, and applicable law, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may process information such as:

- Device identifiers or advertising-related identifiers
- Approximate location, such as location inferred from IP address
- Ad impressions, clicks, and interaction data
- App usage, diagnostics, or crash-related information

The developer does not ask users to submit a name, phone number, email address, or account password in the App. Advertising-related data is handled by Google and its advertising partners according to their policies. Users can manage ad tracking, privacy permissions, and personalized advertising options in iOS Settings.

Google Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Google AdMob Policies: https://support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543
